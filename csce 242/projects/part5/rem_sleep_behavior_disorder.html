REM Sleep Behavior Disorder

REM sleep behavior disorder (RBD) is a conditioned characterized by physically acting out dreams with sounds and movements during REM sleep. This disorder may be associated with other neurological conditions like Lewy body dementia or Parkinson's disease.

Symptoms

  • Movement when sleeping: People with RBD experience various physical movements, like kicking, punching, arm flailing, or jumping from the bed, as a response to action-filled dreams.
  • Noises when sleeping: People with RBD make various sounds, such as talking, laughing, shouting, cursing, or crying, as a response to action-filled dreams.
  • Remembering dreams: People with RBD can vividly recall their dream if they awaken during an episode.

Diagnosis

A sleep medicine physician can diagnose RBD with the help of several diagnostic tools, such as:

  • Physical and neurological exam: A physical and neurological exam will evaluate you for RBD and other disorders.
  • Conversation with sleep partner: A doctor may request your sleeping partner to fill out a questionnaire about your sleep behaviors.
  • Polysomnogram: A polysomnography, or sleep study, is an overnight test performed at a medical facility that meaures brain waves, heart rate, breathing, and leg and eye movements when sleeping.

Prevalence

It is estimated that 0.5 to 1.5 percent of the general population has RBD.
