Insomnia

Insomnia is a common sleep disorder characterized by difficulty falling or staying asleep. Many adults can have short-term insomnia, but some people have chronic insomnia that can last for three months or more.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty falling asleep: People with insomnia find it hard to fall asleep at night and often take a long time to do so.
  • Interrupted sleep: People with insomnia often wake up during the night.
  • Waking up too early: People with insomnia often wake up earlier than desired.
  • Daytime sleepiness: People with insomnia often feel exhaustion during the day due to poor sleep.
  • Memory and mood problems: People with insomnia often feel cranky, depressed, or anxious, and they experience problems with concentration and memory.

Diagnosis

A sleep medicine physician can diagnose insomnia with the help of several diagnostic tools, such as:

  • Physical exam: A healthcare provider can do a physical exam to look for signs of medical problems that may be related to insomnia.
  • Sleep records: A record your sleep patterns for a week or two.
  • Polysomnography: A polysomnography, or sleep study, is an overnight test performed at a medical facility that meaures brain waves, heart rate, breathing, and leg and eye movements when sleeping.

Prevalence

It is estimated that 10% of the general population suffers from chronic insomnia and 20% of the population experiences occasional insomnia symptoms. Women, older adults, and people with a socioeconomic hardship are more vulnerable to insomnia.
