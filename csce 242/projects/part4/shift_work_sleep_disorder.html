Shift Work Sleep Disorder

Shift work sleep disorder (SWSD) is a circadian rhythm sleep disorder characterized by an abnormal sleep/wake schedule. This disorder commonly affects people who work nontraditional hours, like overnight shifts.

Symptoms

  • Insomnia: People with SWSD often have difficulty falling and staying asleep.
  • Excessive sleepiness: People with SWSD often have excessive sleepiness at unwanted times, like during work.
  • Headaches: People with SWSD often have headaches.
  • Issues with concentration and mood: People with SWSD often have difficulty concentrating, decreased alertness, and irritability.

Diagnosis

A sleep medicine physician can diagnose SWSD with the help of several diagnostic tools, such as:

  • Sleep records: A record your sleep patterns for a week or two.
  • Polysomnography: A polysomnography, or sleep study, is an overnight test performed at a medical facility that meaures brain waves, heart rate, breathing, and leg and eye movements when sleeping.

Prevalence

It is estimated that one in five shift workers experience SWSD.
