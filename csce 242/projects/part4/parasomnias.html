Parasomnias

Parasomnias are disruptive behaviors or events that affect a person's sleep. There are three groupings of parasomnias based on the stage of sleep in which they happen: non-rapid eye movement (non-REM) sleep parasomnias, rapid eye movement (REM) sleep parasomnias, and other parasomnias.

Symptoms/Conditions

Non-REM Sleep Parasomnias

  • Sleep terrors: Sleep terrors happen when you wake up suddenly scared. People who have sleep terrors may experience screaming or crying in fright, racing heart rate, diated pupils, fast breathing, and sweating.
  • Somnambulism: Somnambulism, or sleep walking, you may get out of bed and move about while asleep.
  • Confusional arousals: Confusional arousals occur when you wake up extremely confused and disoriented.

REM Sleep Parasomnias

  • Nightmare disorder: Nightmares are vivid dreams that cause feelings of fear, terror, and anxiety.
  • Sleep paralysis: Sleep paralysis is an epsiode where you cannot move or speak while falling asleep or upon waking
  • REM sleep behavior disorder: With this disorder, you act out, vocalize, or make aggressive movements as a reaction to a violent dream.

Other Parasomnias

  • Sleep enuresis: Sleep enuresis, or bedwetting, is the accidental release of pee during sleep.
  • Catathrenia: Catathrenia, or sleep-related groaning, causes you to make repeated moaning or groaning noises during sleep.
  • Exploding head syndrome: Exploding head syndrome causes you to hear a loud noise or explosive crashing sound in your head as you're falling asleep or waking up.
  • Sleep-related hallucinations: Sleep-realted hallucinations cause you to hear or see things that are not there as you're falling asleep or waking up.
  • Sleep-related eating disorder: Sleep-related eating disorder causes you to eat or drink while asleep.
  • Sexsomnia: Sexsomnia is when you carry out seual behaviors during sleep.

Diagnosis

A sleep medicine physician can diagnose parasomnias with the help of several diagnostic tools, such as:

  • Polysomnography: A polysomnography, or sleep study, is an overnight test performed at a medical facility that meaures brain waves, heart rate, breathing, and leg and eye movements when sleeping.
  • Video electroencephalogram or sleep electroencephalogram: These tests allow brain activity to be recorded during sleep.
  • Imaging tests: CT or MRI scans can detect brain degeneration or other possible causes.

Prevalence

It is estimated that 4 to 67% of adults experience some type of parasomnia.
